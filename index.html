Emergency Services > Fire Safety Information

Fire Safety Information

Essential fire safety guidelines and prevention tips

Fire Prevention

Prevention is the best protection against fire. Install smoke detectors on every level of your home and test them monthly. Keep fire extinguishers in key locations and ensure everyone knows how to use them.

Regularly inspect electrical wiring, avoid overloading outlets, and maintain heating equipment. Create and practice a family fire escape plan with two ways out of every room.

In Case of Fire

If you discover a fire, alert others immediately and call emergency services. If the fire is small and you have a clear escape route, you may attempt to extinguish it with an appropriate fire extinguisher.

If evacuation is necessary, stay low to avoid smoke, feel doors before opening them, and never use elevators during a fire emergency.

After a Fire

Do not enter a fire-damaged building until authorities declare it safe. Contact your insurance company and document all damage with photographs. Be aware of structural damage and potential hazards.

Health & Safety > Health Guidelines

Health Guidelines

Current health recommendations and guidelines

General Health Practices

Maintain good personal hygiene by washing hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. Cover coughs and sneezes with your elbow or a tissue, and dispose of tissues properly.

Stay up to date with recommended vaccinations and regular health screenings. Maintain a healthy diet, exercise regularly, and get adequate sleep.

Public Health Measures

Follow current public health advisories and recommendations from health authorities. When ill, stay home to prevent spreading illness to others.

Be aware of health alerts and emergency notifications from PWEA and local health departments.

Mental Health Resources

Mental health is just as important as physical health. Seek support when needed and don't hesitate to reach out to mental health professionals.

Community support groups and counseling services are available through PWEA's community programs.

Health & Safety > Safety Protocols

Safety Protocols

Standard safety procedures and protocols

Workplace Safety

Follow all workplace safety guidelines and use appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE). Report safety hazards immediately to supervisors or safety officers.

Participate in regular safety training and drills. Know the location of emergency exits, first aid kits, and emergency equipment.

Home Safety

Install and maintain smoke and carbon monoxide detectors. Keep emergency supplies including flashlights, batteries, first aid kit, and emergency contact information.

Secure heavy furniture and appliances that could tip over during earthquakes or severe weather.

Public Safety

Be aware of your surroundings in public spaces. Follow posted safety signs and barriers. Report suspicious activities or safety concerns to authorities.

During severe weather or emergency situations, follow evacuation orders and safety instructions from emergency personnel.

Community-based crime prevention program that empowers residents to take an active role in keeping their neighborhoods safe.

Educational programs for children and teenagers covering personal safety, internet safety, and emergency response.

Programs are available for schools, community groups, and families looking to enhance safety education for young people.

Resources > Assistance Programs

Assistance Programs

Support services available to community members

Emergency Financial Assistance

Short-term financial assistance for individuals and families facing emergency situations such as medical emergencies, natural disasters, or unexpected job loss.

Applications are reviewed on a case-by-case basis. Documentation of emergency circumstances may be required.

Temporary Housing Support

Assistance with temporary housing arrangements for those displaced by emergencies, disasters, or other urgent situations.

Coordination with local shelters, temporary housing facilities, and partner organizations to provide safe accommodation.

Resource Connection Services

Help connecting community members with appropriate resources including healthcare services, social services, employment assistance, and educational programs.

Case workers available to help navigate available resources and complete necessary applications.

Legal Aid

Free legal consultation and assistance for urgent legal matters related to emergency situations, including housing rights, employment issues, and benefit claims.
